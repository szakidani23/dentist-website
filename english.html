The importance of oral hygiene

The oral hygiene is essential for healthy teeth. It is the cheapest and most effective way to prevent oral diseases. It prevents tooth decay, diseases caused by bacteria, premature ageing of teeth (periodontal disease) and gingivitis.

Regular brushing is unfortunately is not the same as maintaining good oral hygiene. Many people think that sticking a toothbrush on at night is enough, but it's not.
You do need to visit the dentist from time to time!

Dental implants

A dental implant is actually an artificial root that replaces the root of a lost tooth. It actually resembles a screw, but once healed, it stays in place as firmly as your original teeth.

Cosmetic dentistry

Cosmetic dentistry is an advanced version of aesthetic dentistry. It aims to create the ideal, perfect smile through the creation of flawless dentures. Cosmetic dentistry is a separate discipline with its own vision, methods and tools.

Root canal treatment

A special dental procedure that is the last chance to save a diseased tooth. In this case, the tooth is already dead, but as a result of the treatment it can still remain a full-fledged part of the denture. Typical symptoms are inflammation of the tooth socket and severe pain, which can bring even those who are otherwise afraid of the dentist to the dental office.

Teeth Whitening

Teeth whitening is a chemical process in which the bleaching agent reacts with the substances that stain the teeth. The initiator in the bleaching agent is then activated by a UV light source. The chemical and optical properties of the tooth-colouring chromogens change as a result of oxidation, resulting in beautiful white teeth.

Dental X-rays

Before any dental intervention, it is important that the dentist has adequate information about the current state of the patient's oral cavity so that he or she can make a correct diagnosis. A thorough examination of the oral cavity is not always essential, as the problem is often located in the areas under the gums. X-rays are the best tool to detect this.

Orthodontics

This is usually due to incorrect positioning of the teeth or jawbone. The treatment is not only aesthetic, as an irregular dentition can also contribute to a number of dental, speech and eating problems. This is why it is important to address the issue, either from the time of tooth replacement or in adulthood.

  • The tartar is removed completely painlessly using ultrasonic equipment. The head of the device uses a vibration frequency of 27,000-37,000 Hz to imperceptibly remove the tartar from the tooth surface. The air bubbles created by the frequency also break up tartar that cannot be removed by conventional methods. This procedure does not damage the tooth enamel or the gum. Finally, a soft polishing brush is used to polish the teeth, giving them a shiny, smooth surface that makes it harder for dirt and tartar to adhere. This healthy condition can be maintained over time with regular and correct brushing techniques.

  • If you look in the mirror at the inside of your lower dental arch and see a yellowish or brown discolouration around the neck of the tooth, between teeth or elsewhere, it is most likely tartar. If you notice that your breath is not spotless, or that your teeth bleed when you brush, or even bleed spontaneously, in many cases these may also be due to tartar. If you have a lot of tartar, you may have small white pieces falling off, you may think that small pieces have broken off from one of your teeth, but it may actually be tartar that is breaking up and falling off. Tartar can hide in places where it is not visible at first glance. The persistent presence of tartar can cause gum inflammation, bleeding and premature tooth loss, so it's worth seeing a professional and having regular check-ups.
